The world of Twitch emotes and badges can be very complicated the first

time you have to do it.

Below is a guide on everything you need to know about uploading, swapping

and deleting Twitch badges.

You can find the original guide here.

My personal recommendation will always be to use Manual Mode

as this gives you full control of your emotes as well as the best quality.

How to Upload Twitch Emotes and Badges

To manage your emotes, head to your dashboard.

On the left-hand sidebar, click Viewer Rewards, then Emotes.

Subscriber Emotes is the first section listed on the Emotes page.

Under the Subscriber Emotes section is a list of your current emotes grouped

by tier, as well as your current emote prefix and the progress you've made

towards unlocking your next emote.

To upload an emote, click the + button under the desired tier.

If you do not have any emote slots remaining, you’ll need to remove

an emote before you can upload another.

You can upload your emote in one of two ways:

Auto-Resize Mode

Auto-resize mode is an easy, streamlined way to upload your emotes.

To upload your emote, you'll need a single square image file between

112x112px and 4096x4096px that is under 1MB in size.

This file will then be automatically converted into our other required sizes.

1 | Click the upload icon within the square on the left-hand side

to select the appropriate file. Your emote will automatically be

converted into the required sizes.

2 | Within the Emote Code field, choose a name for your emote (e.g: Love).

Your emote’s full code will be your emote prefix combined with your

emote code (e.g: prefix + Love = prefixLove).

3 | Once you’ve uploaded your files and selected your code, click Upload Emote.

Manual Mode

Deactivating auto-resize mode allows you to upload three separate sizes

(28x28, 56x56, 112x112 px), rather than having them automatically scaled

by the system. If the automatic conversions made via auto-resize mode are not

to your liking and you want more control on each of the sizes, manual mode

allows for image-by-image control for each size. Please note that the three

sizes you supply for each emote must be of the same image and

must each be under 100KB.

1 |Deactivate the Auto-resize toggle and click the upload icon within

each square to select the appropriately sized file (specified underneath the box) for each.

2 |Within the Emote Code field, choose a name for your emote

(e.g: Love). Your emote’s full code will be your emote prefix combined

with your emote code (e.g: prefix + Love = prefixLove).

3 |Once you’ve uploaded your files and selected your code,

click Upload Emote.

---------------------------------------------------

All emotes require manual review and approval before they can be used

on the site. While an emote is pending approval, it will be grayed out,

shown as “pending”, and will not be visible to viewers. To expedite this

process, instant emote upload is available to Twitch Partners in Good

Standing and Affiliates that meet the criteria listed in our Emote Guidelines.

Rearranging Emote Slots

Once emotes are uploaded, you can rearrange their positions within each

tier by dragging and dropping or manually typing in the desired number.

Rearranging your emote positions will affect the order that emotes

are displayed on the site.

---------------------------------------------------

To rearrange your emotes with drag-and-drop:

1 |Mouse over the desired emote.

2 |Click and hold down the mouse button and drag the emote ahead of,

in between, or after any other emotes within a given tier.

3 |Release to save changes.

---------------------------------------------------

For manual rearrangement:

1 |Click on the slot number of an emote and type in

the desired position.

2 |Hit enter to save changes.

Deleting an Emote

1 |Click on the trashcan icon for the emote you would like to delete.

2 |Agree to the prompt to Remove Emote.

Replace One Emote with Another

1 |Follow the instructions above to delete the emote you

wish to replace.

2 |Upload a new emote using the instructions under the

Auto-Resize Mode or Manual Mode sections of this article.

3 |Click Upload Emote.

Modified Emotes

Partners are able to set emote modifiers on their Tier 2 and Tier 3 subscriptions.

Emote modifiers change your existing emotes in fun and interesting ways!

By default, every Partner is assigned the Horizontal Flip modifier as a

Tier 2 benefit as well as Sunglasses and Think modifiers as Tier 3 benefits.

Available to every Partner are the Greyscale, Squished, Horizontal Flip,

Sunglasses and Think modifiers.

---------------------------------------------------

To change your emote modifiers:

1 |Click on any existing modifiers or the + icon to pull up a menu

of options. Note that you can only use each modifier once.

2 |Select the modifier you would like to apply.

You can also clear the modifier completely by clicking on the trashcan icon.

You may also leave one or all modifier slots empty if you'd like to opt

out of offering emote modifiers on a tier.
